The core failure: California Civil Code §8202 instructs property managers to require signed lien releases from subcontractors before approving payments to general contractors. Cindy Rowe received S&W Roof Tear Off's Preliminary Notice on February 4, 2026 — formal legal notice of S&W's lien rights. She did not require a single lien release from RSI Roofing before approving progress payments. The result: a $142,275 mechanic's lien is now recorded against her client's property.

Questions This Raises

These are the questions S&W Roof Tear Off Corporation believes need to be answered.
Whose interests was Kidder Mathews representing?
A fiduciary property manager represents the property owner. Under that obligation, requiring lien releases is standard, non-negotiable practice. The failure to require them — after receiving a Preliminary Notice — creates an uncomfortable question: was Kidder Mathews prioritizing its relationship with RSI Roofing over its fiduciary duty to the property owner?
Was this contract competitively bid?
Commercial property management best practice requires a minimum of 3 competitive bids for a contract of this size ($142,000+). The documented professional relationship between Kidder Mathews Asset Services and RSI Roofing through BOMA San Diego raises the question of whether the property owner received an arms-length procurement process.
Did Kidder Mathews disclose its professional relationship with RSI Roofing to the property owner?
A fiduciary must disclose all material relationships with parties to a transaction. The documented co-presentation by Kidder Mathews Asset Services and RSI Roofing COO at the 2023 BOMA San Diego luncheon represents a professional relationship that should have been disclosed to the property owner when RSI was recommended or selected for this project. Whether that disclosure occurred is unknown.
Was Cindy Rowe aware of RSI Roofing's financial condition and labor litigation?
RSI Roofing has a pending wage and hour class action (Ocegueda v. RSI Roofing, SD Superior Court Case No. 37-2023-00012112, filed March 2023) and a Glassdoor rating of 1.0 out of 5 stars. This information was publicly available before the Via Del Campo contract was awarded. Competent due diligence would have surfaced this information.
