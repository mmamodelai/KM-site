About This Site
We completed the work.
We were not paid.

S&W Roof Tear Off Corporation (CSLB #992187) is an 18-year San Diego roofing subcontractor. In late 2025 and early 2026, we performed commercial roof tear-off work at 16868 Via Del Campo Court, San Diego — a property managed by Kidder Mathews for Sorrento West Properties, Inc., a subsidiary of General Atomics.

We were subcontracted by RSI Roofing (Roofing Services International, Inc.), the general contractor selected by Kidder Mathews. We completed 100% of contracted work. RSI Roofing accepted our work and then failed to pay us $142,275. RSI Roofing has since reportedly closed its doors.

Had Kidder Mathews followed standard property management practice — requiring lien releases from subcontractors before approving progress payments to RSI — this would not have happened. California Civil Code §8202 exists specifically to prevent this scenario. Kidder Mathews chose not to follow it.

Standard of Care
What a competent property manager does.
What Kidder Mathews did.
Industry Standard Practice
  • Conduct competitive bidding — minimum 3 bids
  • Disclose any pre-existing relationships with bidders
  • Require conditional lien releases before each progress payment
  • Require unconditional releases from prior cycle before next payment
  • Verify subcontractors are being paid throughout project
  • Use joint checks when any red flags arise
  • Know your GC's litigation history before engagement
Kidder Mathews — Via Del Campo Court
  • No public record of competitive bidding found
  • Did not disclose BOMA professional relationship with RSI
  • Did not require a single lien release from RSI
  • Did not verify subcontractors were paid before releasing funds
  • RSI collected payments; subcontractor went unpaid $142,275
  • RSI had pending labor class action since March 2023 — public record
  • $142,275 lien now encumbers their client's property
California Civil Code § 8202 — Property Owner Notice
"You can protect yourself from paying twice by requiring your contractor to provide a signed release by the person or firm that has given you this notice before making payment to your contractor."
This language appears on every California Preliminary Notice. S&W filed its Preliminary Notice on February 4, 2026. Kidder Mathews received formal notice of S&W's lien rights. It did not require lien releases from RSI Roofing.

San Diego Housing Commission v. Neil & Kidder Mathews
San Diego Superior Court · Filed August 3, 2021 · Settled August 2022
Settled · $1,000,000

What happened: Kidder Mathews broker Jim Neil was engaged by the San Diego Housing Commission to advise on hotel acquisitions for homeless housing — two deals totaling over $67 million in public funds. While advising the city, Neil secretly purchased 40,000 shares of Chatham Lodging Trust — the counterparty seller on the Mission Valley transaction — before the deal closed. He did not disclose this conflict of interest to his client.

The overcharges: Neil also charged commissions that exceeded his contractual cap of $250,000 by more than double on both deals: $592,500 on the Kearny Mesa acquisition and $502,500 on the Mission Valley acquisition.

The resolution: Kidder Mathews and Neil paid $1,000,000 to settle the lawsuit — $845,000 to the Housing Commission and $155,000 to the City. Jim Neil was permanently disbarred from all San Diego city business. Kidder Mathews accepted a 2-year ban from city contracts.

The pattern: A KM representative had an undisclosed professional relationship with a counterparty. Used their advisory position to benefit that counterparty. The client — a public agency — suffered financial harm. Kidder Mathews paid $1 million to make it go away. The pattern alleged in the Via Del Campo matter is materially identical.

RSI Roofing and Kidder Mathews: A Relationship Beyond Arms-Length
Alleged in S&W Roof Tear Off Corp's formal demand letter, March 9, 2026
Non-Arms-Length

According to S&W's March 9, 2026 demand letter, approximately three years ago RSI Roofing used Kidder Mathews' conference room and Kidder Mathews' legal counsel to conduct the termination of one of RSI's own business partners — Jess David Susi, who was a co-owner and Director of RSI Roofing.

Using a property manager's conference room and attorneys to terminate one's own business partner is not a normal arms-length business interaction. It reflects a level of personal closeness and mutual assistance between organizations that a fiduciary property manager must disclose to clients when recommending or selecting that contractor for work on client properties.

Additionally, Kidder Mathews Asset Services and RSI Roofing COO Melanie Bamba Milinkevich co-presented at the 2023 BOMA San Diego Women in Construction Week luncheon — the same year before the Via Del Campo contract was awarded. This is a documented, public professional relationship between Kidder Mathews' Asset Services division and RSI Roofing's senior leadership. No public record confirms this relationship was disclosed to Sorrento West Properties.

RSI Roofing — Wage & Hour Class Action
San Diego Superior Court · No. 37-2023-00012112-CU-OE-CTL · Filed March 23, 2023
In Settlement — Pending Final Approval

RSI Roofing (Roofing Services International, Inc.) — the general contractor Kidder Mathews selected for the Via Del Campo project — has had a pending wage and hour class action for labor code violations since March 2023. The case reached settlement approval in 2024. RSI Roofing has a Glassdoor rating of 1.0 out of 5 stars as of March 2026.

This litigation was publicly indexed and searchable well before the Via Del Campo contract was awarded. Basic contractor due diligence would have surfaced it. A competent property manager representing a sophisticated client does not award a $142,000+ contract to a GC with active labor class action litigation without at minimum disclosing that fact to the property owner.
